\subsection{Levels of measurement}\label{levels-of-measurement}

\begin{figure}[H]

\begin{minipage}{0.46\linewidth}
\textbf{Nominal Scale Level}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  Categorical data: categories, colors, names, labels, favorite food,
  yes/no
\item
  Not ordered - e.g.~favorite foods \(\blanker{5cm}\)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Ordinal Scale Level}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  Categorical data
\item
  Can be ordered - e.g.~top 5 \(\blanker{5cm}\)
\end{itemize}

\end{minipage}%
%
\begin{minipage}{0.09\linewidth}
~\end{minipage}%
%
\begin{minipage}{0.46\linewidth}
\textbf{Interval Scale Level}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  Numeric data, differences are meaningful, but ratios are not
\item
  Does not have a definite 0
\item
  e.g.~Temperature \(^\circ C\):
  \(80^\circ \text C - 40^\circ \text C = 40 ^\circ \text C\) makes
  sense but \(80^\circ \text C\) is not twice as hot as
  \(40^\circ \text C\)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Ratio Scale Level}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  Numeric data, more calculation possible and ratios make sense
\item
  Has a definite 0
\item
  e.g.~mass, race time, test scores
\end{itemize}

\end{minipage}%

\end{figure}%

\subsection{Frequency}\label{frequency}

Twenty students were asked how many hours they worked per day. Their
responses, in hours, were as follows: 5, 6, 3, 3, 2, 4, 7, 5, 2, 3, 5,
6, 5, 4, 4, 3, 5, 2, 5, 3. Table~\ref{tbl-tbl1} lists the different data
values.

\begin{table}[h]

\caption{\label{tbl-tbl1}Total number of deaths worldwide as a result of
earthquakes from 2000-2012}

\centering{

\begin{tabular}{c | c | c | c}
\textbf{Data Value (hours)} & \textbf{Frequency} & \textbf{Relative Frequency} & \textbf{Cumulative Relative Frequency}\\
\hline
2 & 3 & $\frac{3}{20} \text or .15$ & .15 \\
3 & 5 & $\frac{5}{20} \text or .25$ & .15 + .25 = .40 \\
2001 & 21,357 & 2008 & 88,011 \\
 & & \textbf{Total} & \textbf{832,856}
\end{tabular}

}
