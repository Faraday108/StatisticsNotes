1.1 - Definitions of Statistics, Probability, and Key Terms

  • The science of statistics deals with the collection, interpretation, and presentation of data.

  • In this course, you will learn how to:

  1. organize and summarize data (descriptive statistics).
    • Think creating a graph, or using an average
  2. Use formal methods for drawing conclusions from good data (inferential statistics).
    • Uses probability to determine how confident we are in our conclusions

  • Effective interpretation is based on good procedures for producing data and thoughtful examination of the data.

  • While it may feel there are many formulas, the goal is not to perform numerous calculations, but to gain an understanding of your data.

  • Calculations can be done by a computer, understanding comes from you.

Statistical Models

  • Similar to all other branches of math, statistics uses mathematical models to describe phenomena in the real world. Some mathematical models are deterministic: a value is precisely determined by another value.

Probability

  • Probability is a mathematical tool to study randomness and deals with the chance of an event occurring.
  • If you toss a fair coin 4 times, the outcome may not be two heads and two tails
  • However if you toss the same coin 4,000 times, the outcomes will be close to 50/50.
  • A few repetitions are uncertain, but there is a pattern of outcomes with many repetitions.

Key terms

  • population: a collection of people, objects, etc, under study
  • sample: a portion or subset of the population
  • statistic: a number that represents a property of the sample
  • parameter: a number that represents a property of the population
  • representative sample: a sample that contains characteristics of the population
  • variable: characteristic or measurement that can be determined for each member of a population
    • numerical variable: values with equal units (weight in pounds, time in hours)
    • categorical variable: places person/thing into a category (party affiliation, hair color)
  • data: single value of the variable
Example 1

Determine what the population, sample, parameter, statistic, variable, and data referred to in the following study.
We want to know the mean amount of extracurricular activities in which high school students participate. We randomly surveyed 100 high school students. Three of those students were in 2, 5, and 7 extracurricular activities, respectively.

Solution

  • population: all high school students
  • sample : 100 high school students interviewed
  • parameter: mean amount of extracurricular activities in which all high school students participate
  • statistic: mean amount of extracurricular activities in which the sample of high school students participate
  • variable: let \(X=\) the amount of extracurricular activities by one high school student
  • data: the number of extracurricular activities in which the high school students participate. Includes 2, 5, and 7.
